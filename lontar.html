Traditional Balinese Guide to Understanding Pet Character

Wira, Bhanu, and Bayu - Pawara Team

March 9th, 2025

Lontar Carcan Asu is a traditional Balinese text that deals specifically with dogs. More than a mere record, this lontar is a compilation of in-depth knowledge on various aspects related to dogs, especially in the context of Balinese culture and beliefs. Lontar Carcan Asu serves as a guide to understanding the characteristics, traits and roles played by dogs in the daily lives of Balinese people. It contains detailed descriptions of various types of dogs, each with unique physical characteristics and temperaments. The manuscript also records about 5 different dog traits. In addition to the description of characteristics, Lontar Asu also contains traditional methods for measuring and classifying dog traits based on their physical size.

Determination of dog traits can be measured with the following steps: (1) First, measure the length from the tip of the dog's nose (muzzle) to the tip of the tail. Measure as accurately as possible. We can use a flexible stick, and other measuring instruments. (2) Then, we measure the tip of the nose (muzzle) to the point between the dog's eyes. Then with that measurement, divide the measurement from the muzzle to the tip of the tail by that measurement. (3) Divide the measurements by repeating the order of Paksa, Jaya, Guna, Ketek and Kiul, then see which is the last order of the division.

After taking proper measurements of the dog's body, such as the length of the muzzle to the tail, and the distance between the eyes to the muzzle, the next step is to classify the dog's traits based on the results of these measurements. This division was then linked to the five main traits in Lontar Asu, namely Paksa, Jaya, Guna, Ketek, and Kiul. Each of these traits describes the distinctive characteristics and behavior of the dog according to its body measurements.

Paksa

If it falls in the Paksa order, Dogs with this trait tend to be fierce and aggressive. They are loyal to their owners and guard the home, but are not good with strangers as they can be dangerous. Owners are advised to socialize with these dogs frequently to better recognize strangers.

Jaya

If it falls on Jaya, the dog has a ruler-like character, tends to be spoiled and often whines at its owner. They are more suited as house pets that interact with their owners frequently, rather than house guards. They are loyal companions to their owner's activities, but are difficult to train and require a good approach.

Guna

If it falls in the Guna, the dog will have a submissive character and is very easy to train. Whatever the purpose of keeping a dog, try to keep this type of dog because the dog will be very easy to train. As the name implies, Guna, the dog will greatly benefit its owner.

Ketek

If it falls in Guna, the dog will have an obedient character and is very easy to train. Whatever the purpose of keeping a dog, try to keep this type of dog because the dog will be very easy to train. As the name implies, Guna, the dog will greatly benefit its owner.

Kiul

Lastly, If it falls on Kiul, Dogs with a lazy nature, preferring to eat and sleep. They are suitable for busy owners who don't have much time to spend with their dogs. This dog is more suitable as a house sitter, although it cannot be relied upon for guard duty.
